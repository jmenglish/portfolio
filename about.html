Read on for my journey into UX Design.

I have always been interested in understanding human behavior and motivation. In college, I majored in Psychology after taking many creative art electives. I was unsure of the career path I would follow but hoped to combine psychology and a creative pursuit in some way. After graduation, I spent ten years in project-management-type roles in Technology, Marketing, and Learning Experience Design working with many designers and developers. At this point in my career, I discovered User Experience Design and how this role combined both of my passions and interests. I knew this was the direction I wanted to take my career in and took action by enrolling in Blocâ€™s online Design Track.



In 2018, only half-way into my program I was offered my first job as a UX Designer with ConnectWise. This new role now combines my need to understand others with my desire to find creative solutions. I am learning research techniques from Benchmarking the User Experience, how to use that data to influence change within product design, and balancing this knowledge with the long-term goal of our products and business needs. My design skills are challenged daily to modify my approach and evolve my techniques with each new project tackled. I have room to grow and welcome that opportunity.

What's next?

My long-term goal is to develop mastery within the field of UX Research and Design. The next step in my career will be to attend a graduate program in 2019 while continuing to develop skills in my current role so I can become a lead designer in the near future.
